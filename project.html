§ 01 — The Story

FIVE ACTS.
FOUR CONTINENTS.
ONE TRUTH.

Written by J. Rajmohan Pillai — Rajan's younger brother and the story's narrator. The unique power of this adaptation is that the author was there. He witnessed every chapter. He felt the jealousy, the admiration, the grief. The narrator's complex relationship with his brother is the emotional engine of every scene.

I
ACT I · KOLLAM TO SINGAPORE
THE VENDOR'S
GRANDSON
1948 – 1975
A stamp-paper vendor's grandson leaves Kollam, Kerala with his father's seed capital. He sets up Twentieth Century Foods in Jurong Industrial Estate, Singapore, and creates the Ole brand — the snack brand that dominates Southeast Asia and Japan. His parting words: "Victory will be mine. Loss — Achan's." Ole: cashew nuts, potato chips, mixed nuts — a brand that wins by value addition before India discovers the concept.
II
ACT II · SINGAPORE TO LONDON
THE NABISCO
YEARS
1975 – 1984
Rajan joins Nabisco Brands, moves to London as Area Vice-President, and befriends CEO Ross Johnson (later portrayed by James Garner in HBO's Barbarians at the Gate). Johnson writes on a plaque: "Rajan: a great entrepreneur, executive, person, and most of all — a friend." Rajan then orchestrates the acquisition of Britannia Industries from British hands — returning a 75-year-old Indian institution to Indian control for the first time in its history.
III
ACT III · AT THE SUMMIT
THE BISCUIT
BARON
1984 – 1992
Britannia spans six countries. He brings Coca-Cola back to India via Britco Foods (Britannia-Coke JV). He negotiates Kellogg's India entry. He is on the front page of the Financial Times and Economic Times. The world calls him The Biscuit Baron. But France's BSN Groupe (Antoine Riboud) is slowly, patiently funding his expansion in order to create dependency — then calling in the debt at a moment of maximum vulnerability.
IV
ACT IV · THE FALL
EVERY FRIEND
DISAPPEARS
1992 – 1995
Singapore courts. 29 counts. A 14-year sentence looming. Every powerful friend vanishes. Subha Napoleon — his Singaporean Tamil secretary — furnishes SGD 4.5 million bail alone when all his wealthy allies disappear. He flees to India. He fights in Indian courts funded by his own brother's money. He starts Thai Agri Foods in Bangkok — his last venture — refusing to accept that the fight is over.
V
ACT V · TIHAR JAIL
A WASTED
DEATH
July 4 – 7, 1995
Arrested at Delhi airport. Bail denied by Magistrate M.L. Mehta. Tihar Jail. A vital court letter arrives at the jail and disappears in transit. Jail Doctor Venkatasubbiah arrives more than an hour after the emergency SOS. He leaves DDU Hospital exactly 2 minutes after handover, without briefing the doctors. Rajan Pillai dies on July 7, 1995, aged 46. His mother prays all night for a son already in the morgue. The Leila Seth Commission confirms every failure. Nobody is ever held accountable.

🔑 The Ross Johnson Hook
Ross Johnson — Rajan Pillai's mentor and eventual nemesis — was portrayed by James Garner in HBO's Barbarians at the Gate (1993). That film tells the American side of the RJR Nabisco buyout war. This film tells the Indian side — Rajan's relationship with Johnson from their first meeting to the 29 counts filed against him in Singapore courts.

Marketing line: "The Indian side of the story Barbarians at the Gate never told."
📰 The Darwin Mystery
In 1997, the Straits Times reported that Rajan Pillai had been "sighted in Darwin, Australia" — two years after his documented death and cremation in Kollam. The newspaper claimed the cremated body may not have been his. The mystery was never resolved. This unresolved conspiracy is extraordinary built-in marketing material for the international version.

KERALA STATE GOVERNMENT — FILM INCENTIVES

🏛 KFDC — Kerala
₹2 Cr
KFDC Soft Production Loan
Kerala Film Development Corporation offers a soft loan up to ₹2 Crore at subsidised interest rates for Malayalam-language productions. Repayable over 5 years. Significantly lower interest than commercial film financing. Requires Kerala-registered production company as primary entity.
Eligibility: Kerala-registered company · Malayalam-certified film · Apply: KFDC Head Office, Thiruvananthapuram
🏛 Kerala State — Shoot Rebate
15–20%
Shoot-in-Kerala Production Rebate
15–20% cash rebate on all qualifying production expenditure incurred within Kerala. On a ₹40 Cr production with ₹25 Cr spent in Kerala, this recovers ₹3.75–5 Crore automatically. Covers crew wages, accommodation, location fees, equipment hire, catering, transport.
Eligibility: Min. 50% of total shoot days in Kerala · Expenditure audit required · Apply: Kerala Tourism / Film Office
🏛 Kerala State — Excellence Grant
₹15–25 L

Production grant awarded alongside the Kerala State Film Awards for films of cultural, historical, or social significance. Eligibility: Malayalam-certified film · Submitted for Kerala State Film Awards · Judged on cultural merit

01
Secure Film/Series Adaptation Rights — Urgent
Formal 18-month option agreement (renewable) with the book's author. The author is alive — this is both the project's greatest asset and its most urgent legal priority. Standard option: modest fee + exercise price pre-agreed. Do this before any producer meeting — without it, no deal can progress.

Family Consent — Nina Gopika Pillai & Children
Written consent from Nina Gopika Pillai (widow) and Rajan's children. Without Gopika's consent, her portrayal — she is a major character across 12 of 17 book chapters — creates significant defamation and right-of-publicity exposure in Indian, Singapore, and Thai courts (Bangkok civil case).
03
Media IP Lawyer — India + Singapore Dual Jurisdiction
Engage AZB & Partners or Nishith Desai Associates (India) + Rajah & Tann (Singapore). You need expertise in both jurisdictions because the Singapore Commercial Affairs Department case and the Indian Tihar death involve entirely separate legal systems and standards of proof.
04
Defamation Audit — All Named Real Persons
Multiple real people named: A.K. Dutt (CBI), M.L. Mehta (Magistrate), Dr. Venkatasubbiah, Nusli Wadia, Antoine Riboud, Sunil Alagh, Rakesh Saxena. The Leila Seth Commission report (public judicial record) — quoting it directly is protected speech. Original allegations beyond the record are not.
05
IFTPC + WGA Registration Before First Pitch
Register the treatment and screenplay with Indian Film & Television Producers Council (IFTPC) and Writers Guild of America (West) before the first pitch meeting. Establishes the creation date on record. Protects against script theft during multi-territory simultaneous pitching. Costs minimal fees, takes 48 hours.
06
Danone / Britannia Corporate Risk Review
BSN Groupe is now Danone. Danone partly owns Britannia Industries — one of India's largest food companies. Their legal departments monitor media about corporate history. Show only documented conduct from public company records, published court filings, and the Leila Seth Commission report. No speculation beyond the record.
07
Singapore Commercial Affairs Jurisdiction
Ross Johnson's 29-count complaint and the 14-year Singapore sentence were Singapore court matters. Rajan fled before verdict. Singapore has some of the world's strictest defamation laws. Verify the current legal status of those proceedings before depicting them. Engage Rajah & Tann Singapore specifically for this review.
08
Key Legal Protection — The Leila Seth Report
The Leila Seth Commission Report is a publicly available judicial document produced by a former Chief Justice of a High Court. Every statement in it — including the confirmation of Dr. Venkatasubbiah's "casual and careless" conduct and the finding that A.K. Dutt withheld information — is protected speech when quoted directly and verbatim. Ross Johnson died in 2016: US law cannot protect the deceased.
